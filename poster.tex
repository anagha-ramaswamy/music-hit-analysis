\begin{column}{\colwidth}

  \begin{block}{Introduction}
    Are all hit songs starting to sound and say the same thing? We investigate \textbf{cultural homogenization} in Billboard Hot 100 hits (1970--2023) by combining audio feature extraction (\texttt{librosa}) with NLP on scraped lyrics.
  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Data \& Methods}

    \textbf{4,801 songs} (audio) / \textbf{4,554 songs} (lyrics) across 1970--2023.

    \smallskip
    \begin{tabular}{ll}
      \textbf{Audio} & 8 \texttt{librosa} features; PCA convex hull area \\
                     & \& cosine similarity measure convergence \\[4pt]
      \textbf{Lyrics} & Lexical diversity; LZ77 compressibility; \\
                      & TextBlob sentiment; TF-IDF + LDA topics \\[4pt]
      \textbf{Stats} & Kruskal--Wallis + Mann--Whitney $U$ \\
                     & + Bonferroni (data confirmed non-Gaussian) \\[4pt]
      \textbf{ML} & RF / KNN / LR decade classifier; \\
                  & low accuracy \emph{supports} convergence
    \end{tabular}

  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Audio Feature Trends}
    \begin{figure}
      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/audio_feature_trends.png}
      \caption{All 8 audio features 1970--2023 (mean $\pm$ 1 s.d.). Loudness, energy, and speechiness rise significantly; acousticness declines.}
    \end{figure}
  \end{block}

    Steady convergence through 2000s, a streaming-era expansion in the 2010s, then the sharpest convergence on record in the 2020s.

  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Decade Similarity Heatmap}

    \begin{figure}
      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/decade_similarity_heatmap.png}
      \caption{Cosine similarity of standardized audio centroids. Green = similar; red = opposite.}
    \end{figure}

    The \textbf{1980s and 2020s are acoustically near-opposite} ($r = -0.99$): loudness war ($-23.6 \to -18.7$ dB), rising speechiness, falling acousticness. The \textbf{2010s--2020s are nearly identical} ($r = +0.92$). The \textbf{1990s is the hinge decade}.

  \end{block}

\begin{column}{\colwidth}

  \begin{block}{Lyrical Convergence}

    \begin{figure}
      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/lexical_diversity_analysis.png}
      \caption{Lexical diversity 1970--2023 with quadratic trend ($R^2 = 0.68$, min $\approx$ 2007).}
    \end{figure}

    \textbf{Lexical diversity} (unique/total words) forms a statistically confirmed U-shape ($H = 99.89$, $p = 5.56 \times 10^{-20}$):

    \begin{center}
      \begin{tabular}{lc}
        \toprule
        Decade & Mean Lex.\ Diversity \\
        \midrule
        1970s & 0.383 \\
        1980s & 0.365 \\
        2000s & 0.353 \\
        2010s & 0.342 \textbf{(trough)} \\
        2020s & 0.381 \\
        \bottomrule
      \end{tabular}
    \end{center}

    2020s recovery to 1970s levels confirmed ($p = 1.0$ vs.\ 1970s, Bonferroni).

    \smallskip
    \textbf{LZ77 Compressibility} (structural repetition) spikes in the 2010s (0.848 vs.\ 0.77--0.79 elsewhere; $d = 0.89$) then drops to 0.750 in the 2020s.

    \smallskip
    \textbf{Sentiment} declined chart-wide ($0.165 \to 0.080$), but top-10 hits recover in the 2020s (0.164) --- blockbusters trend happier while the broader chart darkens.

  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Discussion}

    \textbf{Phase 1 (1970s--2000s):} Gradual sonic + lyrical convergence.

    \smallskip
    \textbf{Phase 2 (2010s):} Streaming anomaly --- audio \emph{expands} as more genres chart, yet lyrics peak in repetition and hit lyrical sentiment nadir.

    \smallskip
    \textbf{Phase 3 (2020s):} Partial reversal --- lyrical vocabulary recovers; audio reaches tightest convergence ever. TikTok rewards narrative hooks; production homogenizes around streaming templates.

    \smallskip
    \textit{Caveat: 2020s = only 331 songs (2020--2023).}

  \end{block}
